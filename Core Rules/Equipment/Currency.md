# Currency

## Denominations

1. Velocity uses the standard d20 economy: **copper pieces (cp)**, **silver pieces (sp)**, **gold pieces (gp)**, and **platinum pieces (pp)**.
2. Exchange rate: 10 cp = 1 sp, 10 sp = 1 gp, 10 gp = 1 pp.
3. Item costs throughout [Weapons](Weapons.md), [Armor](Armor.md), and [Gear](Gear.md) are given in these denominations, ported from d20 source material using the same d20-number-direct approach applied throughout.

## Scope

1. Currency represents what it costs a character to acquire an item — nothing more.
2. Currency has no weight, takes no inventory slots, and doesn't interact with the slot-based inventory system in [Inventory](Inventory.md).
3. Tracking how much money a character has is a single number, the same way tracking slots used is a single number.
4. For simplification, this also applies to the contents of a Bottomless Coinpurse — coins, gems, and jewels stored in one have no weight and take no inventory slots. A haul of treasure is just a number while it's in the purse.
5. A **Bottomless Coinpurse** (see [Gear](Gear.md)) is a Bag of Holding for wealth. It accepts coins, gems, and jewels of any quantity and stores them as a single tracked number with no capacity limit. It does not accept other items.

## Losing a Bottomless Coinpurse

If a character's Bottomless Coinpurse is lost, stolen, or destroyed, the wealth inside is not automatically lost — but it is no longer stored conveniently. The character must account for the physical bulk of their wealth until they can replace the purse.

Loose wealth occupies inventory slots as follows:

- One slot per coin denomination currently carried (cp, sp, gp, pp — even a handful counts as a slot).
- Coins of the same denomination stack into a single slot regardless of quantity.
- Gems and jewels occupy one slot per type. Rubies stack with rubies; emeralds stack with emeralds; a single pearl still takes a slot.

A replacement Bottomless Coinpurse costs 50 gp and can be purchased from a general goods merchant, magic shop, or similar vendor (Narrator's judgment for availability).

## Pricing

1. The Narrator sets prices for anything not listed in [Weapons](Weapons.md), [Armor](Armor.md), or [Gear](Gear.md), using those tables as a baseline for relative value.
