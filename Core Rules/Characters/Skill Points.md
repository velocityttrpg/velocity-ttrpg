# Skill Points

## Skill point system

1. Skill points represent a character’s advancement and are awarded only by the Narrator.
2. Skill points are not gained from combat difficulty, repetition, kills, items, abilities, or any source other than Narrator award.
3. Skill points are spent to purchase skills.
4. Skill points may be unspent or spent.

## Skill point awards

1. Skill points are awarded at the end of the session, after play has concluded. Awards are never given mid-session.
2. Attendance awards (optional, decided at session zero for the whole campaign): 1 skill point to every player who attends.
3. Story milestone awards: 1 skill point to every party member when the party reaches a Narrator-defined milestone.
4. Exceptional play awards: 1 skill point to any player whose individual moment elevated the story, at the Narrator's discretion. Not guaranteed every session; multiple players may earn this in the same session.
5. See Awarding Skill Points for full guidance on when and how to award skill points.

## Unspent skill points

1. Unspent skill points are skill points the character has earned but not allocated.
2. Unspent skill points are spent between sessions, outside of play.
3. Unspent skill points may be used to purchase skills.

## Spent skill points

1. Spent skill points are skill points that have been allocated to skills.
2. A character’s total spent skill points determine their effective level.
3. Spent skill points determine which skills a character has unlocked.

## Retraining

1. Retraining allows a character to remove a previously spent skill point.
2. Retraining requires spending 2 unspent skill points to remove 1 spent skill point.
3. Retraining occurs between sessions, outside of play, the same as spending unspent skill points.
4. Retraining requires Narrator approval.
5. When a character reclaims a skill point, they immediately lose all benefits and penalties granted by the removed skill.
