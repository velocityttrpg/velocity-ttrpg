# Dying

## Wounded

1. A character enters the Wounded condition when their Health falls to or below their **wounded threshold** (half their maximum Vitality, rounded up).
2. A Wounded character begins **Bleeding Out**: they lose 1 Health per round at the end of their turn. See Bleeding Out, below.
3. A Wounded character can still attack, defend, cast spells, and take other actions without penalty — they are hurt, not impaired.
4. The Wounded condition ends when the character's Health is restored above their wounded threshold, or when they die.

## Dying

1. A character enters the Dying condition when their Health falls to or below their **dying threshold** (one quarter of their maximum Vitality, rounded up).
2. A Dying character suffers −2 on all Opposition Checks and their movement speed is reduced by 5 ft per AP spent on movement.
3. A Dying character is also Wounded — they are Bleeding Out (if not already stabilized) and all Wounded effects apply.
4. A Dying character can still attack, defend, cast spells, and take other actions — but at reduced effectiveness.
5. The Dying condition ends when the character's Health is restored above their dying threshold, or when they die.

## Consciousness Checks

1. A character who is not unconscious must attempt a Consciousness Check when any of the following occur:
    1. They enter the Dying condition.
    2. They are Dying and take damage.
    3. They are Dying and lose Health from Bleeding Out.
2. A Consciousness Check is a special check, not a standard Opposition Check. The character rolls their dice pool + Willpower. Dice and flat bonuses come from applicable skills. If the result meets or exceeds the damage value that triggered the check, the character remains conscious. If the result is less than the damage value, the character becomes Unconscious.

## Unconscious While Dying

1. A Dying character who fails a Consciousness Check becomes Unconscious. The Unconscious condition's own restrictions control while it is active.
2. An unconscious dying character no longer makes Consciousness Checks but continues Bleeding Out.
3. An unconscious dying character cannot defend themselves and is Vulnerable to attacks.

## Bleeding Out

1. A Wounded character loses 1 Health per round.
2. This loss occurs at the end of the character's turn.
3. If this loss causes the character to enter the Dying condition, they must attempt a Consciousness Check.
4. If this loss reduces their Health further while already Dying, they must attempt a Consciousness Check.
5. If their Health reaches 0, they die instantly.

## Stabilization

1. Stabilization ends the Bleeding Out condition. A stabilized character is no longer Bleeding Out and no longer makes Consciousness Checks.
2. Stabilization does not remove the Wounded or Dying conditions — those end only when Health is restored above the relevant threshold.
3. Stabilization does not remove the Unconscious condition if the character has one. See [Stabilization](Stabilization.md) for the full stabilization process, requirements, and forced awakening rules.
