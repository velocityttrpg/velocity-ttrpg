# Dying

## Dying State

1. A character enters the Dying condition when their Health falls to half their Vitality or lower (rounded up).
2. A character in the Dying condition suffers −2 on all Opposition Checks and their movement speed is reduced by 5 ft. They can still attack, defend, cast spells, and take other actions — but at reduced effectiveness.
3. The Dying condition ends when the character is stabilized (see Stabilization, below), when their Health is restored above their dying threshold, or when they die.

## Consciousness Checks

1. A character who is not unconscious must attempt a Consciousness Check when any of the following occur:
    1. They enter the Dying condition.
    2. They are dying and take damage.
    3. They are dying and bleeding out.
2. A Consciousness Check is a special check, not a standard Opposition Check. The character rolls their dice pool + Willpower. Dice and flat bonuses come from applicable skills. If the result meets or exceeds the damage value that triggered the check, the character remains conscious. If the result is less than the damage value, the character becomes unconscious.

## Unconscious While Dying

1. A dying character who fails a Consciousness Check becomes Unconscious. The Unconscious condition's own restrictions are stricter than Incapacitated's and control while Unconscious is active.
2. An unconscious dying character no longer makes Consciousness Checks but continues Bleeding Out.
3. An unconscious dying character cannot defend themselves and is Vulnerable to attacks.

## Bleeding Out

1. A dying character loses 1 Health per round.
2. This loss occurs at the end of the character’s turn.
3. If this loss reduces their Health, they must attempt a Consciousness Check.
4. If their Health reaches 0, they die instantly.

## Stabilization

1. Stabilization ends the Dying condition and the Bleeding Out condition. A stabilized character is no longer dying or bleeding out.
2. Stabilization does not remove the Unconscious condition if the character has one. See [Stabilization](Stabilization.md) for the full stabilization process, requirements, and forced awakening rules.
