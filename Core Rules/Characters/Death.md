# Death

## Instant Death

1. A character who reaches zero Health dies immediately.
2. A dead character cannot be stabilized.
3. A dead character cannot be forcibly awakened.
4. The dying condition ends immediately upon death.

## Catastrophic Destruction

1. A character who suffers catastrophic bodily destruction dies instantly, regardless of remaining Health.
2. A catastrophically destroyed body cannot be revived by medical or supernatural means.

## Medical Revival

1. A dead character may be revived through medical intervention if they have not been dead for more than one hour.
2. Medical revival requires the body to be sufficiently intact to sustain life.
3. Medical revival requires appropriate medical tools.
4. Medical revival requires Apothecary X and a Master Healer's Satchel.
5. Medical revival restores the character to 1 Health and removes the dead condition.

## Supernatural Revival

1. A dead character may be revived through supernatural means after the one‑hour medical revival window has passed.
2. Supernatural revival is inherently dangerous and may have unintended consequences.
3. Supernatural revival may fail even under ideal conditions.
4. Supernatural revival may produce outcomes that differ significantly from the character’s original state.

## Consequences of Failed Supernatural Revival (Optional)

1. A failed supernatural revival may result in the corpse rising as an undead creature.
2. Possible outcomes include, but are not limited to:
    1. Zombie 
    2. Ghoul 
    3. Revenant 
3. The type of undead created depends on the method of revival, the condition of the body, and the circumstances of death.

## Restrictions on Post‑Death Interaction

1. A dead character cannot take actions.
2. A dead character cannot regain Health except through revival.
3. A dead character cannot be targeted by stabilization or forced awakening.
4. A dead character remains dead until successfully revived.
