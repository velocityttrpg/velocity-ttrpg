# Starting Equipment

## Universal Starting Kit

Every character begins play with the following items at no cost. These are not purchased with starting gold — they are assumed to be in the character's possession at the start of the campaign, provided by the character sheet template.

| Item | Size |
| :--- | :--- |
| Bottomless Coinpurse | Tiny |
| Backpack | Small |
| Bedroll | Small |
| Rope, 50 ft. | Medium |
| Flint | Tiny |
| Rations × 14 | Tiny (stack) |
| Torch × 2 | Tiny (stack) |
| Waterskin | Small |

These items represent the baseline of what any traveling character is assumed to own. No character creation choice affects this list — it applies to every character regardless of lineage, skill investment, or campaign setting.

## Starting Gold

In addition to the universal starting kit, every character begins play with **100 gp** of starting gold, spent before the first session.

Use starting gold to purchase:
- Weapons (see [Weapons](../Equipment/Weapons.md))
- Armor (see [Armor](../Equipment/Armor.md))
- Additional gear (see [Gear](../Equipment/Gear.md))
- Healer's Bags, tools, or other equipment relevant to the character's skill investment

100 gp is enough to equip a medium weapon and medium armor, or a ranged weapon and light armor with gold to spare. It reflects a mid-range adventurer's budget — meaningful choices without implying wealth. Characters cannot buy into heavy armor at character creation regardless of gold; the armor skill requirement gates that independently.

## Notes

1. The Bottomless Coinpurse in the starting kit is the standard item from [Gear](../Equipment/Gear.md) — it holds the character's currency, including their remaining starting gold after purchases.
2. Starting gold not spent before the first session is carried into play as currency.
3. Narrators running campaigns with unusual economic contexts (destitute characters, wealthy noble PCs, etc.) may adjust starting gold with justification.
