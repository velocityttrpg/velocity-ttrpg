# Reactions

## Reactions

1. Reactions are actions taken outside the participant’s turn when a specific trigger occurs.  
2. Reactions cost AP as listed in their descriptions unless a rule states otherwise.  
3. A participant may take at most one Reaction per round unless a rule, skill, or lineage explicitly grants additional Reactions.  
4. A participant may have any number of Reactions available per round, but each Reaction still requires AP to use. Having additional Reactions does not grant additional AP.  
5. Reactions do not change the participant’s position in their side's turn order.  

## Using a Reaction

1. A participant may use a Reaction only when its listed trigger occurs.  
2. A participant must have sufficient AP at the moment the trigger occurs; otherwise, the Reaction cannot be used.  
3. A Reaction must be declared immediately when the trigger occurs. If the participant chooses not to use it, the opportunity is lost.  
4. Reactions resolve immediately after being declared, before the triggering event fully resolves unless the Reaction specifies otherwise.  

## Common Reaction Triggers

1. An enemy moves out of an adjacent space (Opportunity Attack).  
2. A participant is targeted by an attack (Dodge, Parry, Block, or other defensive responses).  
3. A participant within range begins using an ability (Interrupt, Counter, or lineage‑specific equivalents).  
4. A participant enters or leaves a threatened area.  
5. A participant performs an action that explicitly allows a Reaction in response.  

## Reaction Resolution

1. Reactions follow the same resolution rules as Standard Actions.  
2. If a Reaction requires a roll, the roll is made immediately when the Reaction is used.  
3. If multiple Reactions occur simultaneously, the character with the higher Reflex score goes first. If still tied, the players involved decide who goes first; if they can't agree, the Narrator decides.  

## Restrictions

1. A participant cannot use a Reaction if they are unable to act (such as being stunned, unconscious, or otherwise incapacitated).  
2. A participant cannot use a Reaction if the trigger does not occur exactly as described.  
3. A Reaction must be feasible within the current situation; for example, a participant cannot Parry an attack they cannot perceive or Block without a weapon or shield.  
4. Lineage‑specific abilities may impose additional requirements or limitations.  

## Lineage and Skill‑Based Reactions

1. Some lineages, disciplines, or skills grant additional Reactions per round.  
2. These additional Reactions increase the number of opportunities a participant may respond to, but each Reaction still requires AP to use.  
3. Some lineages or skills may also increase a participant’s AP, allowing them to take more Reactions in a round.  
4. Lineage‑specific Reactions follow all core Reaction rules unless the ability explicitly overrides them.
