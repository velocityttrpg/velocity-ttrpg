# Free Actions

## Free Actions

1. Free Actions represent simple activities that require little to no exertion.  
2. Free Actions don’t cost AP unless a rule explicitly states otherwise.  
3. A participant may take any number of Free Actions during their turn as long as they’re reasonable for the situation.  

## Common Free Actions

1. Standing from prone.  
2. Dropping to prone.  
3. Dropping an item or object.  
4. Drawing a weapon.  
5. Sheathing a weapon.  
6. Drinking a potion or liquid.  
7. Tossing an object without attempting to hit a target.  
8. Taking cover within five feet.  
9. Taunting, distracting, or verbally engaging an opponent.  

## Checks And Uncertain Outcomes

1. A Free Action may require an Opposition Check when the outcome is uncertain or dramatically relevant.  
2. The Narrator may call for a roll when the action is simple but the character’s traits, conditions, or the situation introduce meaningful risk.  
3. When a Free Action requires a roll, the roll is made immediately when the action is taken.  
4. A failed roll doesn’t convert the Free Action into a costed action; it simply fails.  
5. A Free Action that meaningfully affects another character (such as intimidation, distraction, or deception) may trigger an appropriate Opposition Check.  

## Restrictions

1. A Free Action can’t replace an action that normally costs AP.  
2. A Free Action can’t be used to interrupt another participant unless it’s also a Reaction.  
3. A Free Action must be physically or narratively feasible within the current situation.
