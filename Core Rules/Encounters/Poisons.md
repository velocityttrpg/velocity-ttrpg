# Poisons

## Overview

Poisons are resolved as **Fortitude checks against a static difficulty** — the same model as Environmental Hazards. The poison sets the difficulty; the character rolls to resist it.

A character exposed to a poison is immediately subjected to the [Poisoned](Conditions.md#poisoned) condition, which defines the round-by-round mechanics. The poison's severity tier determines the check difficulty and the rate of Health loss.

## Delivery Types

- **Contact.** The poison is applied to an object or surface. A creature that touches it with exposed skin suffers its effects.
- **Ingested.** The creature must swallow a full dose — delivered in food or a liquid.
- **Inhaled.** A powder or gas that takes effect when breathed in. Holding one's breath provides no protection; inhaled poisons affect mucous membranes and tear ducts.
- **Injury.** Applied to a weapon, ammunition, or trap component. The creature must take piercing or slashing damage from the coated object to be exposed.

## Severity Tiers

| Severity | Difficulty | Health Loss per Round | Examples |
| :--- | :---: | :--- | :--- |
| **Mild** | 8 | 1 Health | Serpent venom, street-grade sedatives |
| **Potent** | 14 | 1d4 Health | Assassin-grade toxins, exotic animal venoms |
| **Lethal** | 20 | 2d6 Health | Refined creature toxins, alchemical murder tools |

## Notes

1. All poisons are Tiny. Multiple doses of the same poison stack in a single inventory slot (see [Inventory](../Equipment/Inventory.md) §Stacking).
2. Poisons in the Named Poisons table are available as Open Game Content from the d20 SRD; they appear here in Velocity terms.
3. Special conditions listed in the Notes column (Unconscious, Incapacitated, Blind) apply immediately on exposure and last until the poison ends, unless a specific removal condition is stated.
4. The Narrator may introduce original poisons using any severity tier and delivery type. Named poison entries serve as reference points for calibrating new ones.
5. A Master Healer's Satchel used by a character with Apothecary IX neutralizes any poison regardless of severity, ending the Poisoned condition immediately.
