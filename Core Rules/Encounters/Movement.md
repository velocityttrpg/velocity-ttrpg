# Movement

## Movement Basics

1. Movement represents a participant’s ability to reposition during a round.  
2. All movement is measured in feet.  
3. Movement requires spending AP unless a rule, skill, or ability states otherwise.  
4. When a participant spends AP on movement, they gain a movement pool. This pool may be used before, between, or after actions during the participant’s turn.  

## Movement Allowance

1. A participant’s movement allowance is equal to their lineage’s base movement.  
2. Spending 1 AP grants a movement pool equal to the participant’s full base movement.  
3. Additional AP may be spent to increase total movement according to the movement mode being used — see [Movement Modes Table](../Tables/Movement Modes Table.md) for a quick-reference breakdown by mode and AP spent.  
4. Lineage traits, abilities, or conditions may increase or reduce movement allowance.  
5. Movement may be divided around actions unless a rule states otherwise.  

## Standing from Prone

1. Standing from prone is a Free Action.  
2. Standing costs no AP and does not consume movement.  
3. A participant may stand at any point during their turn.  

## Difficult Terrain

1. Movement through difficult terrain is halved, rounding up to the nearest 5‑foot increment.  
2. Difficult terrain includes rubble, deep water, dense foliage, unstable surfaces, or any environment the Narrator designates as obstructive.  
3. Abilities may ignore or reduce the effects of difficult terrain.  

## Forced Movement

1. Forced movement is movement imposed by an external effect.  
2. Forced movement does not cost the target any AP.  
3. Forced movement does not provoke reactions unless a rule or ability states otherwise.  
4. Forced movement may move a participant into or out of melee range.  
5. Forced movement may knock a participant prone if the effect specifies it.  
6. A participant cannot use their own movement to resist forced movement unless a skill explicitly allows it.  

## Movement and Reactions

1. Movement may trigger reactions such as opportunity attacks or abilities that activate when a participant enters or leaves a zone.  
2. A participant may take one Reaction per round unless a rule, skill, or lineage grants additional Reactions.  

## Engagement

1. A participant is engaged when within melee range of an opponent.  
2. Moving within melee range does not trigger reactions.  
3. Moving out of melee range may trigger a reaction if an opponent has an ability that allows it.  
4. Entering melee range may trigger abilities that activate when a participant comes within proximity.  
5. The Narrator determines melee range based on the fiction and the abilities involved.  

## Special Movement Types

1. Some abilities grant special movement modes such as teleportation, shadow‑step, supernatural leaps, flight, gliding, phasing, or intangibility.  
2. Unless otherwise stated:  
    - Special movement does not provoke reactions.  
    - Special movement ignores difficult terrain.  
    - Teleportation cannot be intercepted.  
3. Each special movement ability defines its own limitations and AP costs.  

## Movement and Conditions

1. **Prone:** The participant is prone, takes a −4 penalty on Opposition Checks made to Dodge, Parry, or Block (Dodge remains available — see [Conditions](Conditions.md)), and may crawl or stand.  
2. **Hindered:** The participant has only 1 AP per round and moves at half their normal movement rate.  
3. **Incapacitated:** The participant may crawl (per the Crawling movement modes) but cannot otherwise move under their own power.  
4. **Unconscious:** The participant is prone and cannot move at all — Unconscious's own restrictions are stricter than Incapacitated's and control.  
5. Other conditions may impose additional movement penalties or restrictions.
