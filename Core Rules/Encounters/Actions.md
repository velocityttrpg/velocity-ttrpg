# Actions

## Actions

1. Actions represent discrete activities a participant may perform during their turn.  
2. Each action has an AP cost as listed in its description unless it is a Free Action.  
3. A participant may take actions in any order during their turn as long as they have sufficient AP.

## Action categories

1. Free Actions are simple activities that don’t cost AP unless a rule states otherwise.  
2. Standard Actions are any actions that cost AP, including physical, supernatural, social, or tactical maneuvers.  
3. Reactions are actions taken outside the participant’s turn when their trigger occurs and cost AP as listed.  
4. Movement is purchased with AP and follows the rules in the Movement chapter.

## Common standard actions

1. Making a melee attack.  
2. Making a ranged attack.  
3. Using a supernatural ability.  
4. Shoving, grappling, or attempting to restrain a target.  
5. Disengaging from an adjacent opponent.  
6. Aiming a ranged weapon.  
7. Reloading a weapon.  
8. Taking the Dodge action.

## Action limits

1. Each participant starts their turn with 3 AP. Skills may increase this to a maximum of 5 AP.
2. A participant may take any number of actions during their turn as long as they have AP to pay their costs.
3. A participant may take at most one Reaction per round unless a rule explicitly allows additional Reactions.
4. Unspent AP don’t carry over to the next turn.

## Attacks and strikes

1. An attack is a single action taken by a participant.  
2. A strike is an individual hit roll made as part of an attack.  
3. A single attack may contain one or more strikes.  
4. The number of strikes in an attack does not change the AP cost of the attack.  
5. Multi‑strike actions are attacks that include multiple strikes as part of one fluid motion and cost 1 AP unless they explicitly consume multiple actions. Each strike in a multi‑strike action requires a separate attack Opposition Check and a separate damage roll.
6. A multi‑strike action must be explicitly granted by a skill, ability, or stat block entry. A participant may not declare an ordinary attack a multi‑strike action.

## Multi‑action maneuvers

1. Some actions require more time, commitment, or setup than a standard attack.  
2. These actions consume multiple actions and therefore cost 2 AP or 3 AP.  
3. A multi‑action maneuver represents extended effort, not multiple strikes.  
4. When a participant uses a multi‑action maneuver, they expend the corresponding number of AP from their AP for the round.

## Concentration

1. Concentration is not an action, but it is the only mechanic that can drain AP each round without being one.  
2. See [Concentration](Concentration.md) for full rules on AP costs, Concentration Checks, and the types of effects Concentration maintains.

## Attack cost and multiple attacks

1. Each attack costs 1 AP.
2. Each attack beyond the first made in the same turn imposes a cumulative −2 penalty to that attack's Opposition Check. The first attack is unpenalized; the second is at −2; the third at −4; and so on.
3. This penalty applies to all attacks in a turn, including bonus attacks granted by skills or abilities.

## Attack resolution

1. All attacks are resolved as Opposition Checks. Every participant rolls a minimum of 1d10. Skills that explicitly grant dice add to this base. The attacker adds the relevant Attribute and any flat bonuses from applicable skills. The defender responds with an Opposition Check of their own.  
2. The relevant attack attribute is:  
    - **Strength** for melee attacks  
    - **Strength** for ranged attacks (bows, thrown weapons, crossbows, and similar)  
    - **Willpower** for magical attacks  
3. The defender’s response depends on the type of attack:  
    - **Melee and ranged attacks:** the defender makes an Opposition Check using one of the following, as appropriate to the situation:  
        - **Dodge:** dice pool from Dodge skills + Reflex. The defender rolls clear, twists, or steps out of the path of the attack. Always available. When the defender has no open space to step into (cornered, completely surrounded, restrained, or within 5 feet of the attacker with no open ground), Dodge takes a −2 penalty. A prone character may still Dodge but takes the −4 prone penalty.  
        - **Parry:** dice pool from Parry skills + Reflex  
        - **Block:** dice pool from Block skills + Reflex  
    - **Physical and elemental spells** (fire, lightning, force, cold, and similar — per the spell’s description): the defender may only Dodge. Parry and Block cannot deflect or absorb magic. Standard Dodge rules apply, including the −2 restricted-space penalty and −4 prone penalty. A defender who cannot or chooses not to Dodge takes the hit automatically; armor mitigation still applies.  
    - **Mind-affecting spells** (charm, compulsion, illusion, fear, and similar — per the spell’s description): the defender resists with their dice pool + Willpower. No physical movement can deflect a spell that targets the mind; Parry and Block are unavailable. A defender who cannot make a Willpower check (Stunned, Unconscious, Incapacitated, or similar) takes the hit automatically.  
4. If the attacker’s total is greater than the defender’s total, the attack hits.  
5. If the defender cannot make a defensive Opposition Check at all — because they are Vulnerable (see [Conditions](Conditions.md)), unable to act or react (Stunned, Unconscious, Incapacitated, or similar), lack the AP to pay for a defensive Reaction (see [Reactions](Reactions.md)), or have no Dodge/Parry/Block/Willpower-resistance option available for any other reason — no defensive roll is made. The attack automatically hits. Armor mitigation still applies normally.  
6. On a hit, the attacker immediately rolls for damage and applies the result.  
7. When multiple attacks occur simultaneously, the character with the higher Reflex score goes first. If still tied, the players involved decide who goes first; if they can't agree, the Narrator decides.
