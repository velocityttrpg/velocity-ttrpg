# Concentration

## Concentration Basics

1. Some effects require Concentration to maintain.  
2. Concentration is not an action; it is a maintained state.  
3. A participant may maintain only one Concentration effect at a time unless a rule, skill, or lineage explicitly allows additional effects.  
4. A participant may end a Concentration effect at any time without using an action.

## Maintaining Concentration

1. Concentration effects persist from round to round as long as the participant maintains the state.  
2. Maintaining Concentration costs 1 AP per round, paid at the start of the participant’s turn before any other AP is spent.  
3. If the participant cannot or chooses not to pay the AP cost, the effect ends immediately.

## Losing Concentration

1. Taking damage forces a Concentration Check.  
2. Becoming Stunned, Unconscious, or otherwise incapacitated ends Concentration automatically.  
3. Effects that disrupt focus or impose mental strain may force a Concentration Check if specified.  
4. Forced movement does not automatically break Concentration unless the effect specifies it.

## Concentration Checks

1. A Concentration Check is a Willpower opposition check against the triggering effect.  
2. The triggering effect determines the opposing stat:  
    - Melee attacks: opposed by the attacker's Strength  
    - Ranged attacks: opposed by the attacker's Strength  
    - Supernatural effects: opposed by the attacker's Willpower  
3. Concentration Checks do not cost AP.  
4. On a failure, the Concentration effect ends immediately.

## Concentration and Actions

1. A participant may take actions while concentrating unless the effect specifies otherwise.  
2. Some Concentration effects restrict the types of actions that may be taken (such as requiring one hand free or prohibiting attacks).  
3. A participant may not begin a new Concentration effect while already concentrating unless a rule explicitly allows multiple effects.

## Types of Concentration Effects

1. **Damage‑Over‑Time (DoT):** Applies damage at the start of the participant’s turn while Concentration is maintained.  
2. **Sustained Control:** Maintains zones, barriers, bindings, or other battlefield effects.  
3. **Sustained Buffs or Debuffs:** Maintains enhancements or impairments on targets.  
4. **Sustained Forms or States:** Maintains shapeshifting, flight, cloaking, or other transformations.

## Intense Concentration

1. When a character is fully absorbed in a Concentration effect — committing their attention so completely that they cannot track threats around them — the Narrator may declare the character Vulnerable for the duration (see [Conditions](Conditions.md)).
2. This is a Narrator judgment call based on the nature of the effect and the fiction of the scene; not all Concentration effects qualify.
3. Intense Concentration does not restrict movement.

## Ending Concentration

1. A participant may voluntarily end Concentration at any time.  
2. Ending Concentration does not require an action or AP.  
3. When Concentration ends, all effects tied to it end immediately unless the ability specifies a lingering duration.

## Lineage and Skill Interactions

1. Some lineages or skills may reduce or eliminate the AP cost of maintaining Concentration.  
2. Some lineages or skills may allow maintaining multiple Concentration effects simultaneously.  
3. Some abilities may impose penalties on Concentration Checks or increase their difficulty.  
4. Lineage‑specific Concentration rules override general Concentration rules when stated.
