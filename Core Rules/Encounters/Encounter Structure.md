# Encounter Structure

## Starting an Encounter

1. An encounter begins when the Narrator determines that timing, danger, or opposition requires structured rounds.
2. The Narrator determines side order — see [Turn Order](Turn Order.md) §3.
3. Characters that join the encounter after it has begun act on their side's next turn, in whatever order their side chooses.
    
## Surprise

1. If the Narrator has ruled that the Element of Surprise applies (see [Turn Order](Turn Order.md) §5), a surprise round occurs before the first standard round.
2. Only the surprising side takes turns during the surprise round, if the surprise was severe enough to prevent the other side from acting at all.
3. After the surprise round ends, the first standard round begins.

## Rounds

1. An encounter proceeds in rounds.
2. During each round, the side with priority (per [Turn Order](Turn Order.md)) completes all of its turns, in the order that side chooses, before the other side begins.
3. Characters spend Action Points to move, act, react, and influence the scene.
4. Effects, conditions, and durations advance at the end of each round as specified in their descriptions.

## Ending an Encounter

1. An encounter ends when any of the following are true:

    1. All hostile participants are defeated, incapacitated, or have withdrawn.
    2. One side surrenders or ceases hostilities.
    3. The Narrator determines that the conflict has resolved.

2. When an encounter ends:

    1. Ongoing effects resolve according to their remaining duration.
    2. Conditions persist or expire as specified in their descriptions.
    3. Characters may immediately take narrative actions such as stabilizing allies, securing the area, or regrouping.
