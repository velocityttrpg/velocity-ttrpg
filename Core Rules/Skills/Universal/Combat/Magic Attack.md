# Magic Attack

**Branch:** Combat  
**Tree:** Universal  
**Prerequisites:** A lineage, skill, or campaign setting rule that grants access to magical abilities (see your campaign setting's lineage trees and magic system)

## Description

Magic Attack represents a character's mastery of offensive magic — channeling supernatural power to alter reality and harm a target. The specific tradition (glyphweaving, wavebinding, or whatever form magic takes in a given campaign setting) is defined by that setting's magic system. Every spell cast costs Health, a literal sacrifice of life. Skills in this line unlock spell tiers and improve magical attack and damage rolls. Magic Attack is only available to characters with a lineage, skill, or campaign setting rule that grants access to magical abilities. No Universal spell list exists — available spells are defined by the active campaign setting.

## Notes

1. **Casting a spell hurts you.** Every spell cast costs the caster Health equal to the spell's level (a 3rd-level spell costs 3 Health, a 9th-level spell costs 9, a Level 0 spell costs 0). This is a literal toll on the caster's body, applied immediately when the spell is cast — not an abstract resource cost.
2. This cost is fixed by the spell's level and is **not** reduced by any Magic Attack tier. Investing further in Magic Attack unlocks access to higher-level spells and improves attack/damage rolls, but never makes casting cheaper. The Health cost is the permanent price of admission for magic, regardless of how far a character has invested.
3. See the campaign setting for available spells and any setting-specific flavor on how this cost manifests (e.g., Anar's Magesea/wavebinding toll).
4. **Magic draws attention — but only once it's loud enough.** Level 0–2 spells never draw attention, no matter how many are cast. Starting at **3rd level** (Magic Attack IV), a spell is powerful enough to attract notice, and casting spells in quick succession — multiple casts of 3rd level or higher within the same scene or encounter — risks attracting something to the source of that power. The Narrator should treat repeated casting at this depth as an escalating risk: the more a character casts in a short span, the more likely (and severe) a hostile manifestation becomes, up to and including a creature or entity arriving to attack the caster. This is a Narrator-side judgment call, not a roll or tracked statistic — see the campaign setting's magic system for what specifically answers the call (e.g., Anar's Magesea predators). Overcharging a 3rd-level or higher spell counts as twice as reckless as an ordinary cast of the same level when the Narrator weighs escalation — see §Spell Overcharging.

   **Combat threshold.** If 5 or more spells of 3rd level or higher are cast in total during a single combat encounter — accumulated across all casters from the first round to the last — a hostile manifestation is guaranteed to appear at the end of the round in which the threshold is crossed. The Narrator determines what it is and who it targets; it typically attacks the most recent or most powerful caster, but it is not under any combatant's control. Campaign settings define the nature of the manifestation. Overcharged spells of 3rd level or higher count as 2 toward the threshold.
5. The numbered spell level scale (0–9) is adapted from the d20 System Reference Document's Open Game Content — see the Legal & Attribution page on the Velocity website for the full license notice.
6. **Survival is part of the craft.** At Magic Attack V, the character has trained deeply enough that enduring magic's physical toll is no longer incidental — it's learned. A caster at this depth of investment has conditioned their body to fight through damage that would knock an untrained person unconscious. The +1d10 to Consciousness Checks reflects that conditioning, not a supernatural effect.

## Spell Overcharging

A caster may choose to overcharge a spell at the moment of casting. Overcharging pushes more power through the spell than it was designed to carry, amplifying its effect at a physical cost.

**Health cost.** An overcharged spell costs 1.5× the spell's level in Health, rounded up, instead of the normal cost. Overcharging a Level 0 spell costs 1 Health — the minimum price of pushing any spell beyond its intended limits.

| Spell Level | Normal Cost | Overcharged Cost |
| :---: | :---: | :---: |
| 0 | 0 | 1 |
| 1 | 1 | 2 |
| 2 | 2 | 3 |
| 3 | 3 | 5 |
| 4 | 4 | 6 |
| 5 | 5 | 8 |
| 6 | 6 | 9 |
| 7 | 7 | 11 |
| 8 | 8 | 12 |
| 9 | 9 | 14 |

**Effect.** All numerical aspects of the spell are doubled — range, number of targets, duration, radius, damage, and any other quantified value in the spell's description.

**Attention.** Overcharging a spell of 3rd level or higher counts as twice as reckless as an ordinary cast of the same level when the Narrator judges escalation (see Note 4). Overcharging a Level 0–2 spell does not increase attention risk.

## Training Tier

### Magic Attack I

**Cost:** 1 SP  
**Prerequisite:** A lineage, skill, or campaign setting rule that grants access to magical abilities  
**Effect:** The character may cast Level 0 spells.

### Magic Attack II

**Cost:** 1 SP  
**Prerequisite:** Magic Attack I  
**Effect:** The character may cast 1st level spells. +2 to magic attack rolls.

### Magic Attack III

**Cost:** 1 SP  
**Prerequisite:** Magic Attack II  
**Effect:** The character may cast 2nd level spells. +2 to magic damage rolls.

### Magic Attack IV

**Cost:** 1 SP  
**Prerequisite:** Magic Attack III  
**Effect:** The character may cast 3rd level spells.

### Magic Attack V

**Cost:** 1 SP  
**Prerequisite:** Magic Attack IV  
**Effect:** The character may cast 4th level spells. +1d10 to Consciousness Checks (see Note 6).

## Mastery Tier

### Magic Attack VI

**Cost:** 1 SP  
**Prerequisite:** Magic Attack V  
**Effect:** The character may cast 5th level spells. +1 AP (maximum 5 AP per turn).

### Magic Attack VII

**Cost:** 1 SP  
**Prerequisite:** Magic Attack VI  
**Effect:** The character may cast 6th level spells. +2 to magic attack rolls and magic damage rolls.

### Magic Attack VIII

**Cost:** 1 SP  
**Prerequisite:** Magic Attack VII  
**Effect:** The character may cast 7th level spells. +1d10 to magic attack rolls.

### Magic Attack IX

**Cost:** 1 SP  
**Prerequisite:** Magic Attack VIII  
**Effect:** The character may cast 8th level spells. **Baleful Caster:** when the character rolls damage for a spell, they may reroll the lowest damage die — unless that die already shows its maximum value.

### Magic Attack X

**Cost:** 1 SP  
**Prerequisite:** Magic Attack IX  
**Effect:** The character may cast 9th level spells. +1 AP (maximum 5 AP per turn).
