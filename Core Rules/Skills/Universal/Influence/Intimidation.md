# Intimidation

**Branch:** Influence  
**Tree:** Universal  
**Prerequisites:** Influence I  
**Attribute:** Social

## Description

Intimidation represents a character's ability to project dominance, authority, and threat. It is the art of making someone feel that compliance is their safest option. Intimidation does not require violence — it requires presence. A master intimidator can silence a room with a look or make a hardened criminal reconsider their choices without raising their voice. At mastery, a single character can project that weight onto an entire group simultaneously.
