# Velocity Open License (VOL) — Plain‑English Summary

This is a simple explanation of what the Velocity Open License allows.  
If anything here seems different from the legal text, the legal text wins — but this summary is meant to make everything clear.

## 1. The Core Rules Are Always Free
The basic rules of Velocity will always be free for everyone.  
No one is allowed to sell the core rules or hide them behind a paywall.

You *can* sell things that **use** the rules — just not the rules themselves.

## 2. You Can Sell Your Own Velocity Content
You are allowed to make money from anything **you create** using Velocity, such as:

- adventures  
- settings  
- expansions  
- new archetypes or lineages  
- digital tools  
- supplements  
- fiction  
- artwork  

If it’s your original work, you can sell it.

You just can’t charge people for access to the **core rules**.

## 3. Everything Built on Velocity Stays Open
If you make something using Velocity, you must release it under the same license (the VOL).

This keeps the whole ecosystem open and prevents anyone from locking down parts of the system.

## 4. No DRM or Extra Restrictions
You can’t add DRM, legal barriers, or anything else that stops people from using your Velocity‑based content under the same freedoms you had.

## 5. Attribution Is Optional
You don’t have to credit the original author.  
It’s appreciated, but never required.

## 6. No Warranty
Velocity is provided “as is.”  
If something breaks, causes problems, or doesn’t work the way you expect, the author isn’t responsible.

## In Short
- **Velocity’s core rules are free forever.**  
- **You can sell anything you create using the rules.**  
- **You can’t sell the rules themselves.**  
- **Everything stays open and share‑alike.**  
- **No DRM.**  
- **Credit is optional.**

This license is designed to protect the community, protect creators, and keep Velocity open and thriving — while still allowing you to make money from your work.
