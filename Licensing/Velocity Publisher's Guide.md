# Velocity Publisher’s Guide  
### How to Publish Content Under the Velocity Open License (VOL)

## **1. Purpose of This Guide**
This guide explains how to publish, sell, and distribute content using the Velocity ruleset under the **Velocity Open License (VOL)**.  
It’s designed for creators, studios, and independent publishers who want to contribute to the Velocity ecosystem while keeping it open and thriving.

## **2. What You Can Publish**
Under the VOL, you can publish any original content that builds on the Velocity ruleset, including:

- Adventures and campaigns  
- Settings and worlds  
- Archetypes, lineages, and abilities  
- Equipment, items, and bestiaries  
- GM guides and player supplements  
- Digital tools and web apps  
- Fiction, art, and multimedia content  

You can distribute your work for free or sell it commercially.

## **3. What You Cannot Publish**
You may **not**:

- Sell or paywall the **core Velocity rules** themselves  
- Claim ownership of the Velocity engine  
- Add DRM or legal restrictions that limit others’ rights under the VOL  
- Re-license Velocity content under a different license  

Your work must remain open under the same VOL terms.

## **4. How to License Your Work**
Every Velocity-compatible product must include the following notice:

```
This product is published under the Velocity Open License (VOL).
Velocity © 2026 Michael Hofer. All rights reserved.
For license details, visit [your link to the VOL file].
```

You may also include the **“Compatible with Velocity”** badge on your cover, website, or product page.

## **5. Branding and Presentation**
To maintain clarity and consistency across the ecosystem:

- Use the **Velocity** name only to indicate compatibility (e.g., “Compatible with Velocity”).  
- Do **not** imply official endorsement unless you have written permission.  
- You may use the Velocity logo or badge for compatibility purposes, but not as your own brand mark.  
- Your product should clearly identify your studio or creator name.

## **6. Selling Your Work**
You can sell your Velocity-based content through any platform, including:

- Itch.io  
- DriveThruRPG  
- Patreon  
- Your own website  
- Physical print runs  

You keep all profits from your own work.

## **7. Collaboration and Third‑Party Content**
You can collaborate with other creators freely.  
All contributors must agree to release their work under the VOL.

If you include third‑party material (art, writing, code, etc.), make sure you have permission and that it doesn’t conflict with the VOL.

## **8. Digital Tools and Software**
You may create and sell digital tools that use Velocity mechanics, such as:

- Character builders  
- Skill tree visualizers  
- Inventory managers  
- GM dashboards  
- Virtual tabletop integrations  

These tools must not restrict access to the Velocity core rules.

## **9. Updates and Forks**
You may fork the Velocity ruleset to create your own variant or expansion.  
Your fork must remain under the VOL and keep the core rules free.

You can sell your forked version as long as it follows the same open principles.

## **10. Attribution**
Attribution to the original author (Michael Hofer) is optional but appreciated.  
You may include a credit line such as:

> “Built on the Velocity ruleset by Michael Hofer.”

## **11. Community Standards**
Velocity thrives on collaboration and respect.  
Publishers are encouraged to:

- Credit contributors fairly  
- Avoid plagiarism  
- Respect player safety and inclusivity  
- Keep content accessible and transparent  

## **12. Questions and Support**
For questions, clarifications, or community support, visit the official Velocity channels (Discord, GitHub, or website).

## **13. Summary**
- You can sell your Velocity content.  
- You must keep the core rules free.  
- You must use the VOL license.  
- You can use the “Compatible with Velocity” badge.  
- You keep your profits.  
- You help grow an open, creator‑owned ecosystem.

Velocity is built to empower creators — not corporations.
