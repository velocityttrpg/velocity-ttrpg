Why this page exists

Velocity is intentionally compatible with the d20 System — the ruleset behind 3rd edition Dungeons & Dragons and its System Reference Document (SRD). A handful of Velocity's mechanics are adapted from that SRD for familiarity: the Simple / Martial / Exotic weapon category structure, the 1st–9th spell level numbering used by Magic Attack, and the Challenge Rating–to–Skill Point budget conversion used to port d20 monsters (see Porting d20 Monsters). Everything else in Velocity — its name, its setting material, its skill trees, Opposition Checks, the Action Point economy, and all of its specific text and numbers — is original work by Michael Hofer, published separately under the Velocity Open License (VOL).

The d20 SRD content referenced above was released by Wizards of the Coast under the Open Game License (OGL) v1.0a. That license requires anyone using its Open Game Content to reproduce the license itself and identify what is, and isn't, Open Game Content. This page does that.

Designation of Open Game Content

The following elements of Velocity are derived from Open Game Content and are themselves designated Open Game Content under Section 1(d) of the OGL: the three-tier weapon category framework (Simple, Martial, Exotic) as a generic classification system; the use of numbered spell levels 1 through 9 as a generic mechanic for scaling magical effects; and the general method of converting a Challenge Rating into a points budget for building an equivalent creature.

The specific numbers, dice, skill descriptions, item tables, monster examples, and prose Velocity uses to implement these ideas are Velocity's own original expression and are not Open Game Content — only the underlying generic mechanic identified above is.
