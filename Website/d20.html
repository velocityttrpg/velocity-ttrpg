Velocity and d20

Velocity is not a d20 game.

It does not use a d20 roll to resolve actions. It does not have Armor Class. It does not have character levels, classes, spell slots, saving throws, ability score modifiers, or initiative rolls. None of those mechanics are in Velocity's core, even as options.

What Velocity uses instead:

  • Dice pools built from purchased skills, not a single die with a flat modifier
  • Armor that mitigates damage after a hit lands, not a number that prevents it
  • Skill Points that build a character horizontally — no levels, no classes, no templates
  • Health as the magic resource — casting spells costs you, every time
  • Side-based turn order — no initiative roll, no initiative stat

These aren't simplified d20. They're a different architecture, built for a different priority: cinematic, relationship-driven stories where a tense conversation carries the same weight as a sword fight.

But You Can Bring Your d20 Library

Velocity is designed to consume d20 resources without conversion headaches.

Monsters. Any creature from any d20 bestiary can be ported to Velocity using the SP budget method in the Narrator's Guide. CR maps to a Skill Point budget; the Narrator builds the creature from that budget using the same tools they use for any NPC. Hit points become Vitality. AC becomes armor tier. Attacks become attack skills. Special abilities become unique traits.

Spells. Velocity uses the same spell levels (1st through 9th, plus Level 0). Any stat-based bonus on a d20 spell — attack rolls, save DCs, damage bonuses — maps to Willpower. The spell otherwise works as written.

Equipment. Velocity uses the same weapon categories (Simple, Martial, Exotic) and the same currency (cp/sp/gp/pp). D20 item costs transfer directly.

Campaigns and adventures. The narrative structure of a d20 adventure — encounters, locations, factions, plot beats — ports without translation. Velocity's mechanics resolve the same situations by different means; the story doesn't change.

What This Means Practically

If you own a shelf of D&D supplements, Pathfinder bestiaries, or any other d20 material, that library works with Velocity. You don't need to wait for official Velocity content to cover every monster, spell, or campaign setting. The porting path is explicit, not a workaround.

Velocity just resolves everything differently once the dice hit the table.
