# Paivel
*Shapeshifter — Tracking / Melee Combat*
*True name: Skydancer. Animal form: peregrine falcon.*

> Nobody introduced Paivel to Kalen Thorne. They simply started passing in the same places — a word exchanged here, a message carried there, the kind of acquaintance that grows in the margins. Most of what they know about each other was communicated mind-to-mind. Paivel knows what kind of man Farstrider is. That's why they're here.

## Animal Form: Peregrine Falcon

Paivel's animal form is a peregrine falcon — compact, dark-backed, capable of killing in a dive. It is not a disguise. It is the other half of who Paivel is, and it has opinions.

**Size:** Small | **Fly speed:** 60 ft per AP | **Diving attack:** 120 ft, straight line only  
**Ground movement:** 10 ft per AP

**Attributes (falcon form — independent of human form)**

| Attribute | Score |
|-----------|------:|
| Reflex | 3 |
| Strength | 0 |

**Health:** Shared with human form — one Health pool regardless of form.

**Attacks:**
- Talons (Multi-Strike — two attacks per action, 1d4 each)
- Beak (single attack, 1d4)

**Attack roll:** 1d10 + 3 (Reflex). The falcon attacks with speed and precision rather than mass — it uses Reflex as its attack attribute. Monster exception; does not establish a universal rule. Keen Senses applies to perception and tracking checks only, not attack rolls.

**No armor** in falcon form.

*Talon, beak damage values, and the Reflex-based attack are all Velocity-native — falcon does not appear in the d20 SRD.*

Transformation costs 1 AP and may be performed once per round in either direction.

Cannot speak, use weapons, wear armor, or manipulate objects in falcon form. Can track by scent and sight, fly silently, and use Mindspeech.

## Playing Paivel

Paivel is the party's tracker and scout. Tracking IV means they can identify the maker of any trail — directly useful when hunting Davan Reth in Act 2. Within 30 ft, their Keen Senses make them effectively impossible to deceive through concealment or disguise.

The Social Outcast flaw (−4 on all Social OCs) is not a reason to hide. It is a reason to be deliberate about when to speak. When Paivel stands up in the tribunal hall, they are taking a visible risk. That decision belongs to the player.

**Mindspeech is the adventure's most distinctive tool.** During the Act 2 recess, Paivel can reach Kalen in his holding room without going through any guards. What Farstrider says in that exchange — mind-to-mind, without chains or advocates — is between them.
