## What You Witnessed

You were at the Broken Wheel when it happened. Close enough to the bar to notice things, far enough away that nobody was paying attention to you — which is usually how you prefer it.

The man sat down next to Kalen and leaned in. Kept his voice down. You're good at reading conversations from across a room — posture, timing, the way the other person's face goes still. Whatever he said, he said it like someone delivering a message they already knew would land. A threat. A specific one.

Kalen hit him once, hard, and the man went off his stool. Kalen didn't look back on his way out.

You've provoked people for a living. You know a provocation when you see one. The man on that stool wasn't innocent — he was just slower than expected.

## Playing Cael

Cael is the party's face. A Persuasion total of +7 means he auto-succeeds on every Routine and most Challenging social checks without rolling — save the dice for Dangerous and Extreme situations.

Investigation I gives him (and Sela) access to the full Investigation branch. He can attempt Forensics and Research checks in Act 2 with a +1 flat bonus — he won't always succeed, but he can try.

**Uncanny Adaptability** is the most important ability on this sheet. Save it for a moment that matters: failed tribunal testimony, a critical Persuasion check with the Belar delegation, a Consciousness Check that would end the fight early.
