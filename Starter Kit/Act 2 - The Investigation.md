# Act 2 — The Investigation

## Overview

The players have two hours and two urgent threads: find the Truthstone, find the Truthteller. Both lead to the same place. The Narrator's job is to keep the threads moving, reward players who pursue clever angles, and escalate the sense that time is running out.

**Goal:** Uncover enough evidence to expose Voss, locate Liriel, and act before the tribunal reconvenes.

**Primary skills:** Investigation, Forensics, Research, Tracking, Situational Awareness, Persuasion, Intimidation, Negotiation.

**Ticking clock:** The recess runs two hours. The contract signing is scheduled to follow immediately after — Voss intends to walk from the tribunal to the Counting House the moment the verdict is read. Both the verdict and the signing are the same deadline: the moment Voss has what he came for and can leave Dunmore. The Narrator should introduce at least one reminder that time is moving — a player who passes the Counting House Inn sees the delegation's robes through a window, or a merchant mentions that the bankers have been asking for Voss.

---

## Thread One: Finding Davan Reth

Voss pointed players toward Reth. This is where most groups will start.

### The Broken Wheel Tavern

The barkeep (Odel, weathered, cautious) remembers the brawl. He will talk if approached respectfully or if a player succeeds on a Persuasion check (difficulty 7 — auto-succeed for any character with a flat Persuasion bonus of 7 or higher).

What Odel knows:
- The man Kalen attacked has been coming in most evenings for three weeks.
- He drinks alone, tips poorly, and asks questions about local trade.
- He didn't start the fight with his hands — whatever set Kalen off was words.
- He's been seen leaving in the direction of Millers Lane late at night.

A successful Investigation check (difficulty 8) while looking around the tavern finds a small wax seal impression left on the table where Reth was sitting — the mark of the Holwick Spice Company.

### Tracking Reth

Players who follow the lead to Millers Lane can attempt a Tracking check (difficulty 13) to trace Reth's movements. Success reveals he has been visiting a rented room above a cooperage — not a proper inn, off the books.

The room is currently empty. It contains:
- A letter from the Holwick Spice Company. Read it aloud to the players:

> *Reth — The Dunmore arrangement proceeds on schedule. Confirm full portfolio transfer ahead of the Erevel signing. All inventory lines are to be represented in the documentation — including the Karrath consignment and related interests. The delegation will not inquire beyond what is presented to them. Keep it clean. Complete your work.*

  "The Karrath consignment" means nothing to the players yet. "All inventory lines." "The delegation will not inquire beyond what is presented." A careful reader will feel the shape of something wrong before they can name it. When they later learn what Holwick deals in beyond spices, this letter becomes a document of criminal conspiracy implicating everyone who signed it.

- A list of names with notes beside each. Kalen's entry reads: *"Problem resolved. Tribunal set."* Two other names on the list have been crossed out with no explanation.
- No indication of where Reth currently is.

Reth himself can be found at the docks, arranging passage out of Dunmore for the day after tomorrow. He has not yet left because the contract hasn't been signed — he has to confirm completion before he's paid.

### Confronting Reth

Reth will attempt to bluff, then flee. He will not fight unless cornered with nowhere to run.

If players succeed on an Intimidation check (difficulty 13) or physically restrain him, he will confirm:
- He was sent by the Holwick Spice Company to manage the transition.
- He arranged the charges against Kalen through Voss.
- He does not know where the Truthteller is being held — that was Voss's arrangement, not his.
- What he said to Kalen in the tavern: *"We know about the grain merchant in Serevon. You want that to reach his wife, keep talking."*

This is the provocation. Kalen's contact in Serevon — a source who trusted him — would have been destroyed. Kalen attacked to shut Reth up before he could say more.

## Thread Two: The Truthstone

The Truthstone was removed from the tribunal hall sometime the previous evening. Players investigating its disappearance are pursuing a Forensics thread.

### The Tribunal Hall (After the Recess)

A Forensics check (difficulty 8) on the empty space where the Truthstone sat reveals:
- The stone was removed carefully, not hurriedly — someone knew exactly what they were doing.
- There is a faint trace of wax on the table surface. The same wax used to seal official VTC correspondence.

A successful Research check (difficulty 11) asking around town's administrative offices reveals that the Truthstone is technically the property of the Dunmore Civic Council — but it has been stored in the tribunal hall for so long that most people have forgotten. Its removal would require either authorization from a magistrate or a key that only magistrates hold.

This is circumstantial evidence pointing to Voss. It is not proof on its own.

### Where Is the Truthstone?

The Truthstone is in the Counting House subceller, in the same room as Liriel. Voss needed it gone from the tribunal and didn't want it found anywhere it could be traced back to him. Keeping it with Liriel was a practical decision — one location to guard.

## The Belar Delegation: A Second Path

Players who notice the delegation at the Counting House Inn and succeed on an Investigation check (difficulty 8) know what their presence means: a contract of significant weight is being signed today, in Dunmore, with Belar's seal.

Players who approach the delegation are met with polite, impenetrable reserve. The lead representative (robes, white collar, no jewelry, no name offered) will confirm that they are here on official banking business and that they cannot discuss the particulars with persons not party to the contract.

If players present the letter from Reth's room and explain that the contract being signed was enabled by fabricated criminal charges against a man currently on trial — the delegation's composure shifts. They will not act immediately, but they will delay the signing until they have spoken with Voss personally.

This buys time but does not resolve the conspiracy. The players still need to find Liriel.

---

## Converging on the Counting House

Whether players follow the Reth thread, the Truthstone thread, or both, the trail ends at the Counting House. Voss's guards are positioned at the main entrance.

The subceller entrance is accessible through a locked door behind the main staircase (lock difficulty 14 for a character attempting to pick it, or the key can be taken from one of Voss's guards).

Liriel is being held in the subceller. She is alive, weakened, and — critically — still capable of testifying. She will tell players everything she knows. She knows she was taken by hired men, that the order came from somewhere inside the tribunal, and that the Truthstone is in the room with her.

With Liriel free and the Truthstone recovered, the players have everything they need for Act 3.

## Narrator Notes

**Shapeshifter prejudice makes the investigation harder.** The entire town is reluctant to help exonerate a Shapeshifter. This is not applied as a penalty to the players' check — it is applied as a +4 bonus to the opposition check against them. Any social check to get a townsperson talking about Kalen, his character, or anything that might cast him in a sympathetic light faces active resistance, not just indifference. The difficulty numbers in this document already assume a normal cooperative townsperson; add +4 when the topic touches on Kalen directly.

If Paivel is doing the questioning and her lineage is known to the townsperson, the bonus doubles to +8. A Shapeshifter asking Dunmore to exonerate another Shapeshifter is exactly the kind of thing this town would rather not engage with.

This doesn't mean every townsperson is hostile. It means they're reluctant, and reluctance has a cost. A player who gets what they need anyway has earned it.

**Let players be clever.** If they find an angle not covered here, reward it. The investigation exists to serve the story, not to gatekeep information.

**Don't hide the clock.** Remind players periodically that time is passing. A bell tower that strikes the hour. A townsperson who mentions "the verdict's due soon." Voss passing through the square on his way somewhere he won't explain.

**Voss's behavior during Act 2.** He is composed and helpful if approached again. He will not slip unless a player is specifically watching for inconsistencies and succeeds on the Situational Awareness check (difficulty 14). He does not visit the Counting House while players might observe him — he's too careful for that. But someone may see one of his guards delivering a sealed message to the Belar delegation's inn.

**If players stall or spend the recess on a dead end.** The Narrator does not need to wait for the players to find the right thread. If time is running out and players are stuck, the Belar delegation can arrive at the tribunal hall on their own initiative — a banking delegation witnessing a contract has a professional interest in verifying the parties are in good standing. Their arrival prompts Voss to act, which prompts the confrontation. The players may not have found all the evidence, but Act 3 can still begin. What they found (or didn't find) shapes how much ammunition they have when they get there.
