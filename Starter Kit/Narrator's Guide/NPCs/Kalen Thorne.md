*The accused*
*True name: Farstrider. Animal form: stag.*

## Stat Block

**Lineage:** Shapeshifter  
**Vitality:** 25 | **Health:** 25 | **Wounded threshold:** 13 | **Dying threshold:** 7

| Attribute | Score |
|-----------|------:|
| Strength | 2 |
| Reflex | 1 |

**Skills:** Melee Attack III (light weapons, +2 attack, +2 damage)  
**Armor:** Light (mitigation 3)  
**Weapon:** Shortsword (1d8 + 2)  
**Attack roll:** 1d10 + 4 (Melee Attack II +2, Strength +2)

Kalen cannot act in Act 1 — he is chained and under guard. If freed in Act 3, use the stat block above for human form, or the stag form below if he shifts.

## Stag Form

**Size:** Large | **Movement:** 50 ft per AP | **Armor:** None

| Attribute | Score |
|-----------|------:|
| Strength | 3 |
| Reflex | 2 |
| Fortitude | 2 |

**Health:** Shared with human form — one Health pool regardless of form.

**Attacks (Multi-Strike):** Once per round, Kalen may make a single attack action that strikes twice — antlers and hooves, each with its own attack and damage roll.

| Attack | Roll | Damage |
|--------|------|--------|
| Antlers | 1d10 + 3 (Strength +3) | 2d8 + 4 |
| Hooves | 1d10 + 3 (Strength +3) | 2d6 + 4 |

**Charge:** If Kalen moves at least 20 ft in a straight line before attacking, his antler attack deals an additional 1d12 damage. In a tribunal hall this is almost always available.

**Cannot:** Speak, use weapons or armor, or manipulate objects in stag form. Can use Mindspeech.

**The chains in stag form:** All four legs are bound. He can stand but cannot move or fight. The moment the chains are removed — by a player, by Kalen breaking them with a Strength Opposition Check (difficulty 16) — he is fully mobile.

**Transformation:** Costs 1 AP. May be performed once per round in either direction.

## What He Is

A man who saw something he wasn't supposed to and refused to be bought into silence. A Shapeshifter — which is why the tribunal is proceeding the way it is.

## What He Knows

He saw Voss meeting with Davan Reth on the night in question. He knows what Reth said to him in the tavern — a threat specific enough that he understood exactly what was at stake. He has not told anyone because Reth made clear that talking would cost him more than a tribunal.

## What He Wants

Exoneration. But more than that, he wants someone to believe him.

## How He Plays

Defiant in public, guarded in private. He doesn't trust easily, but responds to players who demonstrate they're trying to help rather than just extracting information.

He submitted to capture deliberately. A Shapeshifter who flees is guilty. A Shapeshifter who *stays* — who stands in chains, refuses to shift, and demands a Truthteller in front of the whole town — is forcing people to look at what's being done to him. The chains are a rhetorical device. He turned his own arrest into an argument, knowing that no one would simply take his word for it.

The Narrator should play him as a man who has thought this through and is holding the line. He is not a victim waiting to be rescued. He is running a strategy — and the players are the piece he needed that he couldn't provide himself.

## The Chains

His hands and feet are both manacled, connected by a short chain that prevents him from fully straightening. In stag form, that means all four legs bound — he can stand, barely, but cannot run, cannot fight, cannot go anywhere. Someone designed these restraints for a Shapeshifter. This was not standard procedure. This was planned.

He could refuse to shift and simply endure. He is. He wants his name cleared, not just his freedom.
