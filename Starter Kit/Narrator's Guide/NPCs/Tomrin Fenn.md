# Tomrin Fenn
*Junior magistrate, Dunmore Tribunal*

## Stat Block

Fenn is not a combatant and will not fight under any circumstances.

**Vitality:** 25 | **Health:** 25 | **Dying threshold:** 13

| Attribute | Score |
|-----------|------:|
| Social | 1 |
| Willpower | 1 |

**Skills:** Investigation I (+1 to all Investigation OCs), Negotiation I (+1 Negotiation).  
**Armor:** None  
**Weapon:** None  
**Negotiation:** 1d10 + 3 (Influence I +1, Negotiation I +1, Social +1)

---

## What He Is

The newest magistrate in Dunmore — appointed eight months ago after three years studying law at the Order of Solan academy in Tuvien. He believes the tribunal process is a civic sacred trust. He is twenty-four years old and has spent most of his tenure being overruled.

He is not naive about people. He is naive about how thoroughly the system can be captured by the people inside it. That distinction matters.

## What He Knows

He knows procedure was violated. He raised it in the pre-session briefing — no Truthteller, no Truthstone, a proceeding of this magnitude — and was shut down twice by Holt ("We don't halt proceedings if there is standing precedent") and once by Voss. He doesn't know *why*. He suspects Voss is complicit in something but has no evidence and no leverage, and he hasn't figured out that Holt is the instrument rather than the principal.

He does not know about Liriel, the Holwick Spice Company, or the Bank of Belar contract.

## What He Wants

He wants someone to tell him what is actually happening so he can do something about it. He has been trying to act correctly inside a corrupted process and finding every door closed. Players who bring him evidence give him something he can use.

## How He Plays

Fenn is a credible witness, not an actor. He cannot intervene in the tribunal directly — Holt outranks him in the procedural chair and Voss's political weight keeps him marginal. What he can do:

- Confirm on the record that Holt bypassed procedure deliberately, not accidentally
- Testify in Act 3 that the pre-session briefing rejected his objections — this corroborates a conspiracy rather than negligence
- Provide the players with a magistrate's stamp of legitimacy if they need to access civic records or request the Voss Trading Company's documents officially

He responds well to players who treat him as a peer. He responds poorly to being managed. If a player tries to tell him what to think rather than giving him facts, he digs in. He has been managed by Voss for eight months and he recognizes the pattern.

**In Act 3:** If Fenn is in the room when Voss is confronted, he is the one who formally moves to suspend the verdict. He doesn't need persuading by that point — he needs the evidence the players have gathered so he can act on it with grounds.
