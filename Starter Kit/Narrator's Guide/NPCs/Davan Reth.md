# Davan Reth
*The Holwick Spice Company's man in Dunmore*

## Stat Block

**Vitality:** 25 | **Health:** 25 | **Dying threshold:** 13

| Attribute | Score |
|-----------|------:|
| Strength | 1 |
| Reflex | 1 |

**Skills:** Melee Attack II (light weapons, +2 attack), Stealth II (+2 Stealth).  
**Armor:** None  
**Weapon:** Shortsword (1d8)  
**Attack roll:** 1d10 + 3 (Melee Attack II +2, Strength +1)

Reth flees before he fights. If cornered with nowhere to run, he will surrender rather than die for the Holwick Spice Company.

---

## What He Is

The Holwick Spice Company's fixer in Dunmore — and not merely for the spice trade. Holwick's routes carry narcotics alongside legitimate cargo. They also move slaves. Reth manages the parts of Holwick's business that cannot appear in a manifest. He has been in Dunmore for three weeks, longer than he has told most people. His job was to ensure nothing complicated the signing before the Bank of Belar delegation arrived — including a Shapeshifter who recognised him for what he was.

## What He Knows

Everything. He orchestrated the charges against Kalen, removed the Truthstone at Voss's direction, and hired the men holding Liriel. He does not know exactly where Voss is keeping the Truthteller — that detail was Voss's arrangement, not his.

## What He Wants

To leave Dunmore before anyone connects him to any of it. The contract hasn't been signed yet, which means he hasn't been paid yet. He is waiting at the docks for confirmation.

## How He Plays

Low-profile and difficult to find. He is not staying at any of the town's inns. Players who investigate the tavern brawl will find witnesses who can describe him. Tracking him down requires a Tracking check (difficulty 13) or persuading the right tavern regular.

Once found, he bluffs first and flees if that fails. A successful Intimidation check (difficulty 13) or physical restraint makes him cooperative.

## What He Said in the Tavern

*"We know about the grain merchant in Serevon. You want that to reach his wife, keep talking."*

Kalen's contact in Serevon — a source who trusted him — would have been destroyed by the exposure. Kalen's attack was not rage. It was the only move that shut Reth's mouth before he could say more.
