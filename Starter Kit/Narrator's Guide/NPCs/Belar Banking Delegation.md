# The Belar Banking Delegation
*Witnesses to the contract*

## Stat Block

Three representatives. Non-combatants. They will not fight under any circumstances.

Interfering physically with a Belar delegation is a diplomatic incident with consequences well beyond Dunmore. The players should understand this without being told directly.

---

## What They Are

Representatives of the Bank of Belar — the politically neutral kingdom whose capital at Erevel sets the world's weights, measures, and currency standards, and oversees international treaties. Banking delegations are dispatched only for contracts of significant financial weight. Their presence in a market town like Dunmore means something large is being signed.

They are identifiable by their uniform: black robes, stiff white collars, no jewelry. Anyone who has traveled widely or invested in Research or Investigation will recognize them immediately.

## What They Want

To witness and formalize the contract between the Voss Trading Company and the Holwick Spice Company, apply the Bank of Belar's seal, and return to Erevel. They have no stake in Dunmore's politics. They do not know the contract was enabled by fabricated charges and an imprisoned Truthteller.

## How They Play

Polite, impenetrable, and impossible to pressure. They do not give their names to strangers. They respond to credible evidence, formal presentation, and institutional language. They respond poorly to accusations without documentation, emotional appeals, and anything that suggests the speaker doesn't understand how the Bank of Belar operates.

If players present credible evidence that the contract was enabled by fraud — specifically that criminal charges were fabricated and a Truthteller was unlawfully imprisoned to prevent testimony — the delegation will halt proceedings immediately. Belar's neutrality is its entire institutional identity. They will not put their seal on a corrupt transaction.

This is a second path to stopping the conspiracy, independent of the tribunal.
