# Aldric Voss
*The corrupt magistrate*

## Stat Block

**Vitality:** 25 | **Health:** 25 | **Dying threshold:** 13

| Attribute | Score |
|-----------|------:|
| Social | 2 |

**Skills:** None (combat). Influence I, Persuasion III, Negotiation II.  
**Armor:** None  
**Weapon:** None

Voss does not fight. If cornered he negotiates, stalls, or flees. His two guards (see Voss's Guards) act on his behalf.

---

## What He Is

Head of the Voss Trading Company — a continent-wide exotic spice merchant whose routes run by land and sea. One of three magistrates sitting on the tribunal. The man behind everything.

## What He Knows That He Wishes He Didn't

The Holwick Spice Company does not only sell spices. Hidden within its trade routes are narcotics and, worse, a slave operation. Voss discovered this after he had already committed to the deal. He told himself it was not his concern. He told himself he was selling a supplier network, not a conscience. He told himself Dardan was worth it.

He has not told himself these things convincingly enough to sleep well. But he has told himself enough to keep going.

## What He Appears to Be

The most reasonable person in the room. In Act 1 he expresses visible discomfort with the missing Truthstone, suggests a delay, and is overruled. He listens carefully when players approach him. He seems like an ally.

## What He Wants

To sign the contract, leave Dunmore, and never look back. He is not a cruel man. He is a man who decided that his comfort was worth more than his principles, and has been making smaller and smaller decisions ever since to protect that first one.

## How He Plays

Measured, helpful, never defensive. He will point players toward a real lead — implicating Davan Reth and the Holwick Spice Company — without implicating himself. He is not a liar by nature. He is a man who made a decision and is managing its consequences with the same efficiency he applies to everything else.

## The Tell

He knows too much about what Reth said in the tavern. If players press him on specifics he shouldn't know, his composure fractures briefly before reasserting. A successful Investigation or Situational Awareness check (difficulty 14) catches it.
