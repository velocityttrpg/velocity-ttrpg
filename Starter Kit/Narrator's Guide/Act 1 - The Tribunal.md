# Act 1 — The Tribunal

## Overview

The players are in the tribunal hall. Kalen Thorne is on trial. The Truthteller is missing, the Truthstone is unavailable, and the chief advocate is pressing forward anyway. Kalen looks at the players and asks them to tell the court what they saw.

Their testimony will not stop the trial. It will create enough doubt — and enough disruption — that the tribunal agrees to a recess before reading the verdict. That recess is the players' clock.

**Goal:** Buy time. Create doubt. Get out of the hall before the verdict is read.

**Primary skills:** Persuasion, Intimidation, Negotiation, Investigation, Situational Awareness.

**Time pressure:** Establish the recess as finite. Two hours is the suggested window — long enough to investigate seriously, short enough to feel the urgency.

---

## Opening Narrative

Read or paraphrase the following aloud:

> The tribunal hall is dark, its vaulted ceiling swallowed in shadow. The entire town seems to have crowded in — shoulder to shoulder, breath to breath — whispering in hushed, uneasy tones. The air is thick with dread, suspicion, and the faint smell of old stone.
>
> A single shaft of sunlight cuts through the heavy drapes, spilling across the center of the chamber like a spotlight. In that harsh beam stands a man in chains, his posture rigid, his chin lifted in defiance. His hands are manacled. His feet are manacled. The chains connecting them are short enough that he cannot fully straighten.
>
> You all know this man.
>
> Kalen Thorne. Friend to some. Rival to others. But today, he is simply the accused.
>
> Three sharp cracks of the chief advocate's gavel snap through the hall. The whispers die instantly.
>
> "Kalen Thorne," the advocate intones, voice cold and clipped. "You stand accused of consorting with enemies of the state. You assaulted a man without provocation. You conveyed civic secrets to Dunmore's enemies."
>
> Her eyes are flat and certain. The kind of stare that has already written the verdict.
>
> "How do you plead?"
>
> Kalen spits at the floor.
>
> "Plead? I plead mockery. Where is the Truthteller?"
>
> A ripple moves through the crowd — confusion, then fear. Someone near you whispers, *"There should be one."* Another voice answers, *"Or at least the Truthstone…"*
>
> The advocate's jaw tightens.
>
> "There will be no Truthteller today. And the Truthstone is… unavailable."
>
> Gasps. A rising wave of unease.
>
> Kalen laughs — a sharp, bitter sound.
>
> "Unavailable. That's one word for it."
>
> He steps forward as far as his chains allow, eyes burning.
>
> "Tell them why the stone isn't here," he snarls. "Tell them what happened to the Truthteller."
>
> The advocate raises her gavel — but a voice from the dais cuts through first. One of the three magistrates leans forward. He is well-dressed, measured, his expression genuinely troubled.
>
> "Chief Advocate Holt." Magistrate Voss. "This is highly irregular. I move that we delay proceedings until the Truthteller can be located."
>
> Holt does not look at him. "Overruled. The tribunal will proceed."
>
> At the far end of the dais, the youngest magistrate — Aldric Fenn, barely past twenty-five, with the look of a man who studied the law because he believed in it — opens his mouth. Holt's glare closes it.
>
> Voss settles back, discomfort visible. He does not press the point. Fenn stares at his hands.
>
> Then Kalen's gaze sweeps the crowd — and lands on you.
>
> "Tell them," he says. "Tell them what you saw."
>
> And just like that — the spotlight shifts.

## What the Players Saw

Before play begins, the Narrator should establish the shared memory. The players witnessed a brawl in the Broken Wheel Tavern the previous night. They saw different things from different positions, but none of them heard what was said before Kalen swung. They know he was provoked. They cannot prove it.

Suggested framing for the table: *"You were all at the Broken Wheel last night. You saw Kalen in a confrontation with a man you didn't recognize — compact, well-dressed, the kind of man who looks like he belongs somewhere more expensive than Dunmore. Kalen went for him first. The man barely defended himself before the barkeep broke it up. You didn't hear what was said. But Kalen's face when he swung — that wasn't anger. That was something colder."*

## Running the Tribunal Scene

### Testifying

Players who stand and speak are making a social Opposition Check — Persuasion or Intimidation, against the tribunal's credibility (difficulty 11 for the crowd; difficulty 14 for Holt directly).

Their testimony is true but incomplete: they witnessed provocation without hearing its content. A successful check creates visible doubt in the crowd. A failed check is dismissed by Holt without ceremony.

Magistrate Voss will quietly support the idea of a recess if enough doubt has been established — his support is genuine-seeming and useful, and should feel like an act of fairness rather than manipulation.

### The Recess

Once at least one player has made a successful testimony check, and Voss has added his voice for delay, Holt reluctantly agrees to a two-hour recess before the verdict is read.

If players fail all testimony checks, the recess still happens — Voss calls for it himself, citing the irregularity of proceeding without a Truthteller. The players get their window regardless, but they've established less goodwill with the crowd.

### Investigating the Hall

Players who look around the tribunal hall during or after the testimony scene may notice:

- The Truthstone's usual place on the advocate's table is empty. There is a ring of dust where it normally sits — it was removed recently. (Investigation, difficulty 7 — auto-succeed for any character with Investigation flat bonus of 7 or higher.)
- Voss watches the players more carefully than the other magistrates do. A successful Situational Awareness check (difficulty 13) catches this.

### Approaching Fenn

Players who approach Aldric Fenn during the recess find a young magistrate who is furious and has nowhere to put it. He believes the tribunal process is being violated. He is correct. He has no power to stop it.

He will tell players: he has never seen a tribunal proceed without a Truthteller or Truthstone. He raised the issue three times in the pre-session briefing and was overruled each time — by Holt, with Voss's silent backing. He doesn't know why, and he doesn't know who to report it to, because the people he would report it to *are* the problem.

He can provide no evidence and take no action. What he can do is confirm, credibly and on the record, that proper procedure was deliberately bypassed. If the players get him to testify in Act 3, his testimony carries weight with the Belar delegation — not as a legal instrument, but as a magistrate acknowledging that the process was corrupted.

Fenn should feel like an ally who is constantly about three steps behind what's actually happening. He keeps trying to do the right thing and keeps finding that the right thing is already foreclosed. Don't let him be pitiable — he's frustrated, not helpless. He just doesn't have the information yet that the players are gathering.

### Approaching Voss

Players who approach Voss during the recess find him cooperative and concerned. He will:

- Confirm that proceeding without a Truthteller is genuinely unusual — he has never seen it done in Dunmore.
- Suggest that the Truthstone's disappearance and the Truthteller's absence are likely connected.
- Point players toward Davan Reth as a person of interest — he describes Reth as "a trader from Holwick who has been in town longer than his business warrants" and suggests the tavern brawl may be connected to whatever Reth is doing here.

This is a real lead. Reth *is* the thread. Voss is giving players something useful while ensuring the trail runs through Reth and stops there.

---

## Ending Act 1

The recess is called. The players have approximately two hours.

Close Act 1 with the following:

> The hall empties in a tide of whispers. Kalen is led back to the holding room — he catches your eye as he goes. He doesn't say anything. He doesn't have to.
>
> Outside, the sunlight is sharp and indifferent. Somewhere in this town, the Truthteller is running out of time.
>
> You have two hours.
