# Act 3 — The Confrontation

## Overview

Act 3 begins when the tribunal reconvenes. What happens next depends on what the players accomplished during the recess — specifically, whether the Belar banking delegation is already in the hall when Voss arrives.

**Goal:** Expose the conspiracy, free Kalen, stop the contract signing, and deal with Voss.

**Primary skills:** Persuasion, Intimidation, Negotiation (social approach); Melee Attack, Ranged Attack, Defense (combat approach).

---

## Voss Arrives

Aldric Voss enters the hall composed. Whatever the players found, whatever they did during the recess, Voss does not yet know the full extent of it. He is a man who has managed difficult situations before. He intends to manage this one.

What he finds when he walks through the door determines everything.

---

## If the Delegation Is Already in the Hall

The Belar banking delegation has presented the documents to Magistrate Holt before Voss arrives. The hall knows.

Voss steps inside and stops.

The silence that greets him is different from the silence of a courtroom waiting for proceedings to begin. He reads it immediately. His eyes move to the delegation. To Holt. To the documents on Holt's table. To the players, if they are visible.

He has two options.

### He Bolts

Some men cut their losses. Voss turns back toward the door — or toward a side exit, or toward the crowd, wherever he judges his best chance to be.

His guards move to clear a path. The crowd begins to scatter. Kalen — still in chains, guards momentarily distracted — shoulders his way forward from his holding position.

**Chase or combat:** See *The Fight* section below.

### He Stays

Voss does not run. He walks to his seat, sets down his effects with deliberate calm, and addresses Holt as though this is a clerical matter that has been handled incorrectly.

He says something measured. Perhaps that this is a misunderstanding. That there are explanations. That the documents have been obtained by parties with an interest in stopping a legitimate business arrangement.

Holt looks at him. She looks at the documents. She does not respond to him.

Every eye in the hall is on Voss. He knows he has lost. He runs.

**Chase or combat:** See *The Fight* section below.

---

## If the Delegation Is Not in the Hall

Voss does not know the players went to the delegation, or does not yet know what the delegation was shown. He regains his composure in the corridor, straightens his coat, and enters the hall as though nothing has happened.

He takes his seat. He asks Holt to call the proceedings back to order. He requests that the accused be brought before the tribunal for summary judgment.

What happens next depends on the players.

### The Delegation Arrives

If the players went to the delegation during the recess and showed them the documents, the delegation is on their way. They arrive before Holt can pass judgment — the doors open mid-proceedings, and the lead representative walks to the front of the hall without pausing.

She presents the letter and the draft contract to Holt with both hands, as though serving a formal filing. She explains, in the level cadence of someone reading from a ledger, what the Karrath consignment is. What Class K inventory means. What the two crossed-out names on the accompanying list imply about how Holwick resolves complications. She notes, without editorial comment, that Aldric Voss committed to this contract with full knowledge of its contents.

Holt reads the documents. The hall is silent.

Every eye turns to Voss. Proceed to *Voss Breaks*.

### The Players Present Evidence

If the players did not go to the delegation, they must act now. They have until Holt is ready to pass judgment.

**With Liriel:** Producing Liriel creates its own disruption — no roll required. The crowd knows her. The silence that falls when she enters is not shock; it is recognition, followed by something that sounds like relief.

Faeries cannot lie. This is not a cultural norm or a matter of reputation — it is what they are. What Liriel says in this hall carries the weight of established fact. She needs no stone, no oath, no corroboration.

She states what she knows: that she was taken by hired men, that the order came from inside the tribunal, and that she has been held in the subcellar of the Voss Trading Company's Counting House.

If Voss is within ten feet of her when he responds, she will know immediately whether he is telling the truth. She will say so, quietly, without theater: *"That is a lie."* She cannot do otherwise.

Every eye turns to Voss. Proceed to *Voss Breaks*.

**With documents only:** If the players have the letter, the draft contract, or the route map, they can demand to be heard. This requires interrupting proceedings — either a successful Intimidation check (difficulty 14) or a Persuasion check directed at Holt specifically (difficulty 11, since Holt is a zealot for process and a request to examine documentary evidence is, technically, proper procedure).

Once Holt has the documents in hand, she reads them. The hall is silent. Every eye turns to Voss. Proceed to *Voss Breaks*.

**With Reth:** If the players captured Reth and brought him to the hall, he can be compelled to testify. Reth is not loyal to Holwick or to Voss — he is loyal to his own survival, and he will read the room quickly. A successful Intimidation check (difficulty 11, lower if he is restrained or if Liriel is visibly present) is enough to put him in front of the tribunal.

If Liriel is also present, Reth cannot lie. He knows it. He will not try. What follows is not dramatic — it is methodical and devastating. He names Voss. He names the Karrath waystation. He names what "Class K inventory" means and how it moves. He names the two crossed-out names on the list and what happened to them.

Voss does not wait for Reth to finish. Proceed to *Voss Breaks*.

---

## Voss Breaks

Voss has been composed throughout — cooperative, reasonable, the most measured person in the room. That composure has been doing enormous work.

It doesn't hold.

Not in a dramatic outburst. In something quieter and more damning: a stillness, a careful breath, a glance toward the door that tells everyone in the hall exactly what kind of man he is.

He stands. He says something measured — perhaps that this is a misunderstanding, that there are explanations, that the players have been manipulated.

If Liriel is present and within reach, she looks at him. She does not need to speak. The crowd knows what she is. They are watching her face.

Then he moves for the door.

**If both Reth and Liriel are present:** Voss does not attempt composure. The moment he sees both of them in the same room, he understands that every lie he could tell will be named immediately by a Truthteller, and the man who could contradict the testimony is standing right there. He skips straight to the door. The Narrator does not need to give him a speech — the run *is* the speech.

---

## The Fight

Voss's two guards move to clear his path. If the players do not act immediately, Voss gets through them and into the street.

**The Narrator should decide, based on the table's energy and remaining time, which version plays out:**

### Option A: The Hall

The crowd scatters. Kalen — still in chains, guards momentarily distracted — shoulders into the fight from his holding position.

This is short, chaotic combat in a confined space. Three to four rounds. The goal is not to kill anyone — it is to stop Voss before he reaches the door.

**Enemies:** Six to eight guards — scale to match the number of players at the table (one or two guards per player is the target). These are Voss's hired men stationed at and around the tribunal hall; they use the same stat block as Voss's Guards. They are not the same guards as the two at the Counting House. Voss knew today might go wrong. He brought enough.

Voss will not fight; he is trying to move.

**Win condition:** Voss is physically stopped, or his guards are incapacitated, or a player blocks the doorway and wins an Intimidation check (difficulty 11) that makes the guards reconsider.

### Option B: The Street

The chase is a series of Athletics checks (Strength-governed) against Voss's flight (difficulty 12). Players who fail fall behind; players who succeed cut off his route.

If the delegation has not yet arrived at the hall — because the players went straight back rather than following the delegation in — Voss runs directly into them on the street. The lead representative steps into his path. She says nothing. She holds the documents.

The delegation stopping Voss mid-flight requires no additional combat.

---

## Resolution

With Voss stopped and the evidence on record, the tribunal has no choice.

Holt does not fight it. She is a zealot for process, and the process has spoken. She formally withdraws the charges against Kalen. She does not apologize. She does not look at Voss.

Kalen's chains come off.

The Belar delegation issues a formal notation that the Voss Trading Company transaction is suspended pending investigation. The Common & Crown Trading Company does not exist today. Whether it ever will is a question for another time.

Voss is taken into custody by the tribunal's own guards — the same guards who were, minutes ago, his employees.

---

## Aftermath

**Kalen Thorne** shakes the players' hands or doesn't, depending on his relationship with each of them. He is not a man given to speeches. What he says, or doesn't say, is worth a moment.

**Liriel** — if present — is escorted from the hall by townspeople who know her. She will stop at the door and look back at the players. She doesn't need to say anything either.

**Davan Reth** — if not already in custody — will be found at the docks the next morning, still waiting for a ship. The contract's collapse means he was never paid. He is, in the end, easy to catch.

---

## Narrator Notes

**The confrontation should feel earned.** Act 1 built sympathy and doubt. Act 2 built evidence. Act 3 is where it lands. Don't rush the tribunal scene for the sake of getting to the fight. The fight is the punctuation mark, not the sentence.

**Voss is not evil.** He is a man who made a choice and then made every subsequent choice to protect the first one. Let that come through in how he plays in this scene — not as a cartoon villain, but as someone who genuinely thought he could manage this, and is only now discovering he couldn't.

**Liriel's testimony needs no corroboration.** Faeries cannot lie — it is not a reputation, it is what they are. If a player asks why the hall simply accepts her word, the answer is that they always have. See *Core Rules/Lineages/Faerie.md* and *The Truthstone.md* for the full rule.

**If Sela enters blood rage during the fight.** Once she's Wounded (Health 13 or lower), Sela must roll 1d10 + 2 against difficulty 20 at the start of each turn — she will almost certainly fail. When she does, she attacks the nearest target regardless of allegiance. The Narrator does not take over the character; the *player* declares the attack, targeting whoever is closest. The fight becomes two problems simultaneously: stop Voss and protect Sela from herself. If an ally needs to physically restrain her (a grapple, a Strength Opposition Check), that is a valid action. The blood rage ends when combat ends or her Health is restored above 13.

**Award Skill Points at the end of the session.** Suggested awards: 1 SP to every player (attendance/completion), 1 additional SP to any player who drove a pivotal moment in the investigation or confrontation. See the Narrator Quick Reference for full guidance.
