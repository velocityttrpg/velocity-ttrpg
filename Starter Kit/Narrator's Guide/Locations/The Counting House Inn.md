# The Counting House Inn

## Description

Dunmore's best inn — which is to say, comfortable rather than fine, with clean rooms and a landlady who asks no questions of guests who pay in advance. The name is coincidental to the VTC building two streets away; it predates the Voss Trading Company by thirty years.

The Belar banking delegation has occupied the inn's three best rooms since their arrival two days ago. They take their meals privately. They have received no visitors. The landlady will say only that they paid a month in advance and have been no trouble.

## Key NPCs Present

- **The Belar Banking Delegation** — three representatives in black robes and stiff white collars, currently in their rooms or taking a private meal
- **The landlady, Maret** — practical, discreet, will not discuss her guests without good reason

## What Players Can Find Here

**From Maret** (Persuasion, difficulty 8):
- Three guests arrived two days ago, paid a month in advance, cash
- They dress identically — black robes, white collars, no jewelry
- She has never seen their kind in Dunmore before
- They asked, on arrival, for directions to the Voss Trading Company Counting House

**Recognising the delegation** (Investigation or Research, difficulty 8 — auto-succeed for any character with relevant flat bonuses or the Voracious Mind trait):
The uniform is the Bank of Belar's. Banking delegations from Belar are dispatched only to witness contracts of significant financial weight. Their presence in Dunmore means something large is being signed today.

**Approaching the delegation directly:**
They are polite, impenetrable, and will not discuss their business with persons not party to the contract. However, players who present credible evidence — specifically the letter from Reth's room or the draft merger contract from the Counting House subcellar — will find the lead representative's composure shift in a way that Maret's composure does not.

The delegation will not act immediately on a verbal accusation. They will act immediately on documentation. Once they have seen the letter referencing the Karrath consignment, or the Class K inventory clause in the draft contract, the lead representative stands, collects the documents, and walks to the tribunal hall.

She does not send word ahead. She goes herself.

## The Delegation as an Investigation Path

Players who go to the delegation before finding the physical evidence will be politely turned away. Players who go after finding it will find an audience that is quiet, precise, and extremely motivated. Belar's institutional reputation cannot survive having its seal on a slaving operation. The lead representative understands this within seconds of reading the documents.

This is the second path to exposing Voss — and it leads to the same room. The delegation presenting fraud evidence to Magistrate Holt in open tribunal is as public as Liriel's testimony. Kalen's charges cannot survive either one. Voss cannot survive both.
