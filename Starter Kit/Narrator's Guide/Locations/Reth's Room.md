# Reth's Room
*Above the cooperage on Millers Lane*

## Description

Not an inn room — a private arrangement, paid in cash, with a cooper who asks no questions. A single room above the barrel storage, accessible by an external stair. It smells of fresh wood shavings and something underneath that doesn't belong in a cooperage. The window looks out onto the lane.

Reth is not here. He has not been back since the tribunal was called this morning. He left in a hurry but not in a panic — the room is untidy but not ransacked.

## Finding the Room

Players following the lead from the Broken Wheel Tavern know to look on Millers Lane. A Tracking check (difficulty 13) traces Reth's movements from the tavern to this building. Alternatively, asking the right questions of the right tradespeople on Millers Lane (Persuasion, difficulty 8) turns up a cooper who will admit, reluctantly, that he has a lodger.

## What Players Can Find Here

**The letter** (no check required once the room is found):

> *Reth — The Dunmore arrangement proceeds on schedule. Confirm full portfolio transfer ahead of the Erevel signing. All inventory lines are to be represented in the documentation — including the Karrath consignment and related interests. The delegation will not inquire beyond what is presented to them. Keep it clean. Complete your work.*

The Holwick Spice Company seal is on the broken wax at the letter's fold.

**The list** (no check required): A list of names in Reth's handwriting. Most entries have brief notes. Kalen Thorne's entry reads: *"Problem resolved. Tribunal set."* Two other names are crossed out with no explanation. No dates. No indication of what "resolved" meant for them.

**A second document** (Investigation, difficulty 8 to notice it's been folded into the lining of a saddlebag): A partial route map. Several waypoints are marked in a different ink from the rest — added later, in a different hand. One waypoint is labelled *Karrath*. It is not on any legitimate trade route between Holwick and Dunmore.

## Atmosphere

The room feels like a place someone intends to leave. The saddlebag is already packed. Whatever Reth is waiting for — confirmation that the contract has been signed, word that the problem has been fully resolved — he has no intention of staying in Dunmore once he gets it.
