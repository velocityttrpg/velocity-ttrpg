# The Voss Trading Company Counting House

## Description

A well-kept, respectable-looking building two streets from the tribunal hall — the kind of building that has never needed to look like anything other than what it claims to be. The VTC crest is carved above the door. The ground floor smells of ink, wax, and old ledgers.

Ground floor: a reception area, two clerks at standing desks, shelves of bound correspondence organized by trade route and year. Everything is orderly. Everything looks legitimate. It is legitimate — the spice business is real, the ledgers are accurate, and the clerks know nothing about what is in the subcellar.

The subcellar entrance is a locked door behind the main staircase, half-hidden by a standing shelf of archived correspondence.

## Key NPCs Present

- **Two guards** — stationed at the main entrance, 3 AP per turn, light armor
- **Two clerks** — non-combatants, know only the legitimate side of VTC business. Will not obstruct players forcefully but will send for a guard if they feel threatened.
- **Liriel** — in the subcellar, weakened but alive

## Accessing the Subcellar

**The locked door** (lock difficulty 14 to pick). The key is carried by the senior guard. It can be taken by force, lifted with a Stealth check (difficulty 13) while distracted, or obtained by incapacitating or persuading the guards.

**The guards** can be bypassed with a Persuasion or Intimidation check (difficulty 13). They are professionals, not loyalists — if the situation is clearly lost they will not fight.

**Falcon approach**: The upper-floor windows are accessible from the outside. A character in animal form — or with appropriate climbing skills — can enter from above and reach the staircase without passing the front entrance.

## The Subcellar

Stone walls, low ceiling, a single lamp burning low. Cold. The Truthstone sits on a shelf to the left of the entrance, wrapped in cloth. Liriel is against the far wall, her hands bound, her luminescence dimmer than it should be.

She is aware, alert, and immediately cooperative. She will tell the players everything she knows.

**The Truthstone** is intact and functional.

## What Players Can Find Here

**In the ground-floor correspondence** (Research, difficulty 13): Trade records for the VTC's legitimate spice routes — supplier names, route maps, pricing agreements. Nothing incriminating on its own, but cross-referenced with the letter from Reth's room, the Karrath consignment appears as a recurring entry under "specialty freight" with no further description.

**In the subcellar** (no check required): The Truthstone. Liriel. And, tucked behind the shelf that conceals the door: a sealed document bearing both the VTC crest and the Holwick Spice Company crest — an early draft of the merger contract, including a clause referencing "the full transfer of all active inventory management agreements, including those designated Class K." Class K is not defined in the document.
