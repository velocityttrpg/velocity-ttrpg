# The Broken Wheel Tavern

## Description

A working tavern — not rough, not fine, exactly what it needs to be for a market town. Mismatched chairs, a long bar, the smell of woodsmoke and yesterday's stew. The broken wagon wheel mounted above the door has been there so long nobody remembers the story behind it.

This is where the brawl happened. The table Kalen and Reth were sitting at is still slightly off-true from being shoved during the fight. The barkeep has not moved it back.

## Key NPCs Present

- **Odel** — the barkeep. Weathered, cautious, not unfriendly. He sees everything that happens in his tavern and has opinions about most of it. He will talk if approached respectfully; a Persuasion check (difficulty 7) is only needed if players are clumsy about it.
- **Three regulars** who were present the night of the brawl. They remember Reth. They do not know his name.

## What Players Can Find Here

**From Odel** (Persuasion, difficulty 7 — auto-succeed for Cael):
- The man Kalen attacked has been drinking here most evenings for three weeks
- He sits alone, tips poorly, asks questions about local trade — who ships what, which routes are reliable, who owns what
- He didn't start the fight with his hands. Whatever set Kalen off was words — Odel didn't hear what was said, but he heard the tone
- After the brawl, the man left in the direction of Millers Lane

**From the regulars** (Persuasion, difficulty 8, or simply asking the right questions):
- One can describe Reth precisely: compact, well-dressed for Dunmore, the kind of man who looks like he belongs somewhere more expensive
- Another noticed he met briefly with a second man two nights before the brawl — outside, in the alley beside the tavern, after dark. The second man left quickly. The regular didn't get a good look.
- The third overheard Reth asking the barkeep which ships were currently docked and when they were departing

**Physical evidence** (Investigation, difficulty 8): A small wax seal impression left on the table where Reth was sitting — the mark of the Holwick Spice Company. Faint, probably from sealing a letter. Easy to miss unless someone is looking.

## Atmosphere

The tavern is quieter than usual today. Most of its regulars are at the tribunal hall. The few who remain speak in low voices. The brawl is not what people are talking about — the missing Truthteller is.
