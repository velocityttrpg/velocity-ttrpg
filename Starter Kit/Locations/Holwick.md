# Holwick

*Narrator-only background. Players should not read this document.*

---

## The Town

Holwick is a trading town roughly two days' ride from Dunmore, larger and more prosperous. It sits at the junction of two trade roads and has a functioning river port — enough infrastructure to move goods quietly in several directions at once. It has the kind of civic respectability that comes from money, and the kind of moral flexibility that comes from having held it long enough to stop worrying about where it came from.

Most of Holwick's citizens have no idea what the Holwick Spice Company really is. To them it is the town's most successful export business, a source of civic pride, and a reliable employer. The company's leadership has always been careful to keep it that way.

---

## The Holwick Spice Company

On paper: a well-established trading company dealing in common and specialty spices, serving markets from common kitchens to noble tables. Their routes run by land and sea. Their reputation for reliability is genuine — the legitimate side of their business is run professionally, because it has to be. It is the cover.

Their plan to acquire the Voss Trading Company's exotic supplier network is real and commercially sound. Merging VTC's high-end noble contacts with Holwick's common-market reach would create exactly the continent-spanning spice business The Common & Crown Trading Company is meant to be. The Bank of Belar's involvement is legitimate. The delegation in Dunmore is not aware of anything beyond the spice trade.

---

## The Hidden Operation

Holwick's trade routes also carry narcotics — substances that move disguised as exotic culinary ingredients, sold through the same channels as legitimate spices. This is profitable and largely invisible. It is not the thing that brings the King's justice.

The slavery is.

Holwick has, for at least a decade, used its shipping infrastructure to move human cargo alongside legitimate goods. The operation is carefully compartmentalised — only a handful of people within the company know the full picture. Manifests use coded language. "Live cargo" and "the Karrath consignment" appear in internal correspondence; to an outside auditor they could mean livestock or specialty goods requiring careful handling.

The Karrath consignment refers specifically to a holding point — a waystation on one of Holwick's sea routes where people are held before transfer. Players who investigate the letter in Reth's room will find the phrase. They will not know what it means until they do.

---

## What the Merger Would Have Meant

The Common & Crown Trading Company, backed by the Bank of Belar, would have had:

- Continental reach through both land and sea routes
- Belar's institutional seal — making it effectively immune to casual scrutiny
- VTC's exotic supplier relationships, adding noble-table access to Holwick's common-market infrastructure
- A respectable face so thoroughly established that no one would think to look behind it

For Holwick's leadership, this was not merely a business expansion. It was the acquisition of untouchability. A Belar-sealed company operating at continental scale, dealing in goods from the humblest kitchen to the highest court, with the King's own trade inspectors waved past at the border.

The slave operation, folded into that infrastructure, would have become effectively invisible.

---

## Key Figures

**The company's leadership in Holwick** remains unnamed in this adventure — they are a distant threat, not an immediate one. Davan Reth is their man in Dunmore. If the players expose the conspiracy and the King's justice falls, it falls on Reth first and travels up the chain from there.

**Davan Reth** knows the full picture. He has been with Holwick long enough to be trusted with the parts that don't appear in any legitimate document. He is not a true believer in anything — he is a professional who has decided that this particular employer pays well enough to justify the work.

---

## What Happens When It Unravels

If the players expose the conspiracy — through the tribunal, through the Belar delegation, or by getting word to civic authorities — the consequences extend well beyond Dunmore:

- The Bank of Belar immediately withdraws from all proceedings and issues a formal inquiry. Their institutional reputation requires it.
- The King's investigators become involved. A slaving operation with documented ties to a Belar-endorsed company is a diplomatic and criminal emergency.
- The Holwick Spice Company faces dissolution and criminal prosecution. The Common & Crown Trading Company never comes into existence.
- Voss faces the King's justice for knowingly facilitating the deal. His cooperation with investigators is his only remaining leverage.
- Reth, if captured, becomes the most valuable person in the investigation. He knows names, routes, and locations — including the Karrath waystation.

The players will not see most of this play out during the adventure. But the Narrator should know that what they set in motion in Dunmore does not stop at Dunmore's borders.
